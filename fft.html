Fast fourirer transform Explained

definition:

Fast fourier transform is a discrete fourier transform which uses a divide and conquer approach and hence is faster
time complexity of FFT is O(nlogn)

Concepts:

A signal has different kinds of representations
1) coefficient representation
2)point value representation
3)Vector representation
all these forms are internconvertible
A signal can be considered as a polynomial equation
eg:1 - 5 x + x² 3
the highest power in the polynimial equation is called degree of polynomial here it is 2
On X-Y plane, if we evaluate this equation for different values of x on X-axis,we will get different values of equation for Y-axis
eg: for x=1 equation 1 - 5 x + x² 3 will become 1-5(1) + (1²)*3=-1
hence it will create a point on XY plane (1,-1)
This method of evaluating the coefficient form of a signal against different values of x is called "Evalation" and the inverse of the process is called "Interpolation"
Evaluating a coefficient form of a signal against (degree + 1) values of x to get (degree + 1) points gives us a point value representation of the signal
Converting coefficient form to point value form takes multiplication of atleast (degree - 1) multiplications for (degree + 1) values of x
hence the time complexity of thiis process of converting a coefficient form of signal into point value form is O(n² )
